Both birds were first described by Linnaeus (Linné) in 1758. They prefer mixed forest divided by meadows and especially mires. These kind of habitats are disappearing in Europe, so the grouse population is also decreasing. Lapland’s forests and mires provide & ensure them a large and possibly the best habitat to flourish.


Did You Know?

Rackelhahn or Rackelwild is the sterile hybrid created between mating Western Capercaillie (Wood Grouse) & Black Grouse. The male being more similar to Black Grouse while the female to Wood Grouse.


Wood Grouse (Tetrao urogallus) is the largest bird in the forrest. An adult capercaillie can weigh as much as 5 kilo. Old legends said that the witch could appear in the form of a black grouse or in the shape of a grouse.
