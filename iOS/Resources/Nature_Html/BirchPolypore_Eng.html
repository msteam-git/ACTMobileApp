Birch Polypore grows on dead or dying birch. It is attributed with medicinal, at least antibacterial, properties. You can easily find it on all our trails. In traditional medicine birch polypore extract is used because of its anti-hemorrhagic, anti-parasitic and anti-cancer properties. But there is one organism it unfortunately kills...... the birch!

Did You Know?
Two pieces of birch polypore were found on a necklace of the famous Ötzi, the Iceman, a 5000 year old mummy discovered in the Italian – Austrian Alps.
